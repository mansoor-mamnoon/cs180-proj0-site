Project 0: Becoming Friends with Your Camera

Shot with the default iPhone camera.

CS180/CS280A Due Sep 2, 2025 at 11:59 PM Mansoor Mamnoon
I wanted to see how much moving my feet changes a photo. Same phone. Same lens. Three small experiments and a few notes on what I noticed.

Part 1 – Selfie: Wrong vs Right

Story: I started with the classic arm-length shot, then I walked back a few meters and matched the framing. That tiny change fixed most of what I didn’t like about the first photo.

Part 2 – Architectural Perspective Compression

Story: I kept the building about the same size across both shots and only changed where I stood. Seeing the sign shift in relation to the wall made “compression” click for me.

Part 3 – Dolly Zoom

Move back and keep the subject size

Story: I walked back step by step and kept the cups the same size in frame. The background stretches while the stack holds steady. It felt strange while shooting and fun to see as a loop.

Takeaways

  • Distance sets perspective. Framing decides what we emphasize.
  • For people shots I’ll step back first, then frame.
  • For scenes I’ll choose distance to control how flat or deep it feels.
